# All Things New: The Returning King and the World Made Whole

**Weeks 50–52**

## Overview

In three final weeks you will gather the threads of every covenant and see them woven into a single vision of completion. The Old Testament does not end in silence — it ends in expectation. Isaiah sees a feast on a mountain where death is swallowed up. Daniel sees a Son of Man receiving an everlasting kingdom. Zechariah sees a day when the LORD will be king over all the earth. These eschatological texts look beyond exile, beyond restoration, beyond anything Israel had yet experienced, toward the day when all things are made new. The garden becomes a city, the curse is reversed, and God dwells with his people face to face.

## The Foundation

The biblical narrative spans from garden to city. A single figure appears throughout: the one who walked with Adam in the cool of the day is the one who will descend from heaven with a shout.

Each covenant contains unfulfilled promises awaiting completion:

- The serpent not yet finally destroyed
- Final judgment still pending
- Not every knee yet bowed
- Death still present
- Enemies of the Davidic King not yet his footstool
- Obscured spiritual vision still in effect

The consummation represents when all pending realities become present. It completes rather than departs from the overarching narrative — the final movement of a symphony that has been building since the first note.

## In Your Study

In the NT companion study, the Olivet Discourse (Matthew 24–25) describes Christ's return and final judgment. 1 Corinthians 15 develops resurrection theology — death's defeat and the transformed body. 1–2 Thessalonians addresses the Lord's descent and the day of the Lord. Revelation 15–22 concludes with the new heaven, new earth, and the Lamb's eternal reign.

## The Full Circle

The narrative returns to its beginning, transformed. Eden's garden becomes new Jerusalem's city. God's direct presence with humanity — barred since Genesis 3 — returns. The tree of life beside the river of life bears abundant fruit, "its leaves for the healing of the nations." Absence marks the final state: no curse, no death, no sorrow.

Each covenant reaches completion:

- **Creation:** renewed
- **Adam:** serpent destroyed
- **Noah:** humanity saved through final judgment
- **Abraham:** uncountable multitude from every nation
- **Moses:** law internalized
- **David:** eternal reign established
- **New Covenant:** face-to-face dwelling with God, eternally
