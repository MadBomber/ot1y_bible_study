# Jesus: From Genesis to Revelation

**A One-Year Old Testament Chronological Bible Study**

> *The Old Testament is not a prelude. It is the first act of a single story -- and the central figure of that story is already present on every page.*

This study traces Christ through the entire Old Testament, organized around seven major covenants and a final consummation. The purpose is singular: to expose how Jesus is everywhere in Scripture -- not as an afterthought imposed on ancient texts but as the one to whom every promise, every sacrifice, every prophet, and every king has been pointing from the beginning.

Fifty-two weeks. Five days per week. 260 individual studies. From "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth" to the last whisper of expectation before the silence breaks and an angel appears to an elderly priest in the temple.

## How This Study Works

Each week contains:

- **Overview** -- theological context, key themes, and Christ connections for the week's readings
- **Five daily studies** -- Historical Context, Christ in This Day (the centerpiece), Key Themes, Connections, Reflection Questions, and Prayer
- **Discussion guide** -- opening question, review, 12-16 discussion questions organized by daily reading, Going Deeper, Application, Closing Prayer, and Looking Ahead
- **Memory verse** -- one verse per week with connections to each day's reading

The arc moves chronologically from creation through the prophets, but the lens never changes: every page points to Christ. The "Christ in This Day" section in each daily study is the most important section -- it draws explicit lines from the Old Testament text to the person, work, and mission of Jesus, quoting specific New Testament passages that connect back to the reading.

## The Thread

The study follows a single thread from beginning to end:

- **Genesis 1** -- The Word speaks creation into existence (John 1:1-3)
- **Genesis 3:15** -- The seed of the woman will crush the serpent's head
- **Genesis 22** -- The ram substituted on Moriah (John 1:29)
- **Exodus 12** -- The Passover lamb whose blood covers (1 Corinthians 5:7)
- **Leviticus 16** -- The Day of Atonement (Hebrews 9:11-12)
- **2 Samuel 7** -- The throne that will never end (Luke 1:32-33)
- **Isaiah 53** -- The servant who bears our iniquities (Acts 8:32-35)
- **Daniel 7** -- The Son of Man receives an everlasting kingdom (Mark 14:62)
- **Jeremiah 31** -- The new covenant written on hearts (Luke 22:20)
- **Isaiah 65** -- New heavens and a new earth (Revelation 21:1-5)

The first verse of the Old Testament and the first verse of John's Gospel are the same story, told from two sides of the incarnation.
