# Creation Covenant: Christ as the Word and Creator

**Weeks 1–2**

## Overview

Before anything was made, the Word already was. In two weeks you will explore the opening act of all Scripture — God speaking the universe into existence through the same Word who would one day take on flesh. Genesis 1–2 reveals not merely how the world began but *who* began it: the Creator whose character is stamped on every element of order, beauty, and goodness in the cosmos. Paul would later declare that "all things were made through him, and for him" (Colossians 1:16). The story of redemption begins here — not with a problem to solve, but with a Creator who makes everything good.

## The Foundation

Christ existed before creation as the creative agent. John's Gospel positions Jesus as present at creation: "All things were made through him, and without him was not any thing made that was made."

Creation reflects Christ's character through order, beauty, and goodness. Paul describes Jesus as "the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of all creation" (Colossians 1:15-17), framing Genesis 1 as a Christological event. The world was made by him, for him, and through him.

## In Your Study

In the NT companion study, John's prologue (John 1:1-18) declares the Word as Creator. Hebrews 1–2 presents the Son as the one through whom God made the world, and Hebrews 8–12 reveals his glory as both creator and sustainer.

## Looking Ahead

The Creator will become the Re-Creator, fulfilling original creation's purpose through restoration rather than replacement, as indicated in Revelation 21:5: *"Behold, I am making all things new."*

## The Personal Dimension

God did not create humanity in the aggregate — he formed *a man* from dust and breathed into *his* nostrils. Before there was a nation or a people, there was a person, known and placed in a garden by name. The creation covenant is personal before it is cosmic: you are not an accident of matter but a bearer of the divine image, deliberately made and purposefully placed. The God who spoke galaxies into existence also knows the number of hairs on your head (Matthew 10:30). Every covenant that follows builds on this: *the Creator knows you personally.*

> "For you formed my inward parts; you knitted me together in my mother's womb." — Psalm 139:13
