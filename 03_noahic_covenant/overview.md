# Noahic Covenant: Judgment and Salvation

**Weeks 5–8**

## Overview

In four weeks you will witness God's devastating response to a world consumed by wickedness — and his merciful rescue of one righteous family through an ark. The flood is Scripture's first great picture of judgment and salvation arriving together: the same water that destroys also delivers. After the waters recede, God establishes his first formal covenant — *berith* — with Noah and all creation, sealing it with a rainbow. From there the narrative follows humanity's scattering at Babel and the table of nations that sets the stage for God's next move: calling one man out of Ur.

## The Foundation

Humanity's wickedness prompted God to judge the world through a flood while preserving Noah's family via an ark. "Every intention of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually" (Genesis 6:5).

God's response was dual: devastating judgment paired with merciful salvation. Noah, a righteous figure in a corrupt generation, was commanded to build the ark. The covenant's scope is universal, covering all creation and all descendants. The rainbow serves as a sign of God's promise.

Central insight: **Judgment and salvation are not opposites but companions.**

This is Scripture's first covenant using the term *berith*, showing God's commitment to preserving creation for his redemptive plan.

## In Your Study

In the NT companion study, 1 Peter 3:18-22 and 2 Peter 2–3 develop the flood as a type of baptism and final judgment. Jesus references Noah's days in the Olivet Discourse (Matthew 24–25). Hebrews 11:7 honors Noah's faith.

## Looking Ahead

The covenant guarantees God preserves the world — but only temporarily. Peter connects the flood to final judgment when the heavens dissolve, yet righteousness follows. The ark prefigures Christ; the rainbow symbolizes mercy outlasting wrath.

## The Personal Dimension

In a world where "every intention of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually," one man was different: "But Noah found favor in the eyes of the LORD" (Genesis 6:8). The covenant with all creation began with one person's faith. Noah believed God's warning about things not yet seen, and by that faith he built an ark and saved his household (Hebrews 11:7). The flood narrative asks the most personal of questions: *when the world around you is going one direction, will you go with God?* Salvation came through one door of one ark — and it came to those who walked through it.

> "By faith Noah, being warned by God concerning events as yet unseen, in reverent fear constructed an ark for the saving of his household." — Hebrews 11:7
